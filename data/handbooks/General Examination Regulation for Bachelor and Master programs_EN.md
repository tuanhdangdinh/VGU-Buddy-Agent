## CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS

## Article 1. Scope of Adjustment

- (1) This general examination regulation applies to all programs in both Master's and Bachelor's degrees of the Vietnamese-German University (VGU) after transition and is supplemented by specific examination regulations for each degree program. Degree  programs,  in  which  students  obtain  the  degree  of  a  German  Partner University before transition, are regulated solely by the examination regulation of the  partner  university.  For  joint  degree  programs,  after  transition,  conjoint examination regulations issued by both universities are applied.
- (2) Based  on  this  general  examination  regulation,  individual  specific  examination regulations shall be created for each degree program to define content and program related issues and requirements more precisely. The individual module descriptions  become  part  of  the  specific  examination  regulations.  Admissions requirements are determined in the admission regulations.
- (3) If  there  are  discrepancies, this  general examination regulation takes precedence over the examination regulations for the specific degree program.

## Article 2. Aims of the Programs

Bachelor's  degree  graduates  possess  a  broad  knowledge  of  the  basic  science,  the practical applications and a critical understanding of the subject. They know the most important theories and methods and can apply them. They can take responsibility in teams of experts and define goals for working and learning processes. They are able to develop new solutions and defend their arguments towards experts.

Graduates  of  the  Master's  degree  programs  have  a  comprehensive,  detailed  and specialized  knowledge on the latest  state  of  knowledge  of  the  discipline.  They  have technical and conceptual skills for solving problems, especially strategic problems. They will be able to lead teams in the framework of complex tasks and present their work's results. They can define new applications or research-oriented tasks under reflection of the social, economic and cultural impact.

## Article 3 . Academic Degrees

Following the successful completion of studies, graduates will be awarded the academic degree by the VGU and in the case of double or joint degree programs, the academic degree of the German partner university.

## Article 4. Examination Board

- (1) The  Examination  Board  in  this  general  examination  regulation  is  the  relevant competent Examination Board of the faculties of the VGU.

- (2) The Examination Boards of the faculties of the VGU are responsible if their own programs  are  affected.  The  Examination  Boards  of  the  partner  universities  are responsible for the framework of their degree programs offered at the VGU. The Examination Boards will cooperate with each other.
- (3) For the organization of examinations and the tasks assigned by this examination regulation, each faculty of the VGU forms an Examination Board for its programs. The Examination Board consists of the chairman, the deputy, five official voting members and three other non-voting substitute members. The chairman, the deputy and two other members are elected from the group of academic staff (professors, lecturers and research and teaching assistants), one member from the Department of Academic and Student Affairs and two members from the group of students; each  group  (Academic  staff,  Department  of  Academic  and  Student  Affairs, Students) has a substitute member. Each group shall nominate candidates with no more than twice the number of members represented on the Examination Board. The term of the members from the groups of VGU's staff shall be three years, the term of the student members one year. Re-election is possible.
- (4) All members of the Examination Board are elected by the Faculty Council. As long as no Faculty Council is established, the Faculty Board consisting of the Dean and Vice-Deans nominates the members of the Examination Board. If several faculties are involved in the study programs, the faculties will agree on the composition of the Examination Board. The elections can be conducted in secret or by open ballot, depending on the respective faculty regulations. In case it is  not regulated, the elections are secret. In order to avoid the need for by-elections during the term of office, substitute members from each group are also elected. If a board member is prevented from attending a meeting or leaves the Examination Board permanently during his term of office, the substitute member automatically takes his place on the board. The board members elected by the Faculty Council must be informed without delay. The election shall be deemed as accepted if it has not been rejected in written form within two weeks.
- (5) The Examination Board constitutes a quorum (of at least five members) when in addition to the chairman or its deputy, at least two further voting members of the group of academic staff or their representatives and at  least  two further voting members  or  their  representatives  are  present.  Decisions  are  made  by  simple majority.  In  the  case  of  a  tie,  the  vote  of  the  chairman  decides.  The  student members and substitute members of the Examination Board are not involved in the recognition of examinations and the appointment of examiners and assessors. The Examination Board may meet by videoconference.
- (6) The  Examination  Board  shall  ensure  that  the  provisions  of  the  examination regulations are adhered to and ensures the proper conduct of examinations. It is, in particular, responsible for deciding on  appeals against decisions taken in examination  procedures  and  the  compliance  with  deadlines.  In  addition,  the Examination Board regularly, at least once  a  year, has to report to the Faculty

Council and the German partner universities on the development of examinations and average duration of study. This report shall be published in an appropriate manner. It makes suggestions for the reform of the examination regulations and the  study  course  plan  and  discloses  the  distribution  of  grades  and  the  overall grades. The Examination Board may delegate its tasks for all normal cases to the Chairman.  This  does  not  apply  to  decisions  on  appeals  and  the  report  to  the faculties.

- (7) The  official  members  of  the  Examination  Board  have  the  right  to  attend  the examinations.
- (8) Examination  board  meetings  are  not  public.  The  members  of  the  Examination Board and the representatives are subject to official secrecy. If they are not VGU's employees, they shall be bound to secrecy by the chairman of the Examination Board.
- (9) The Examination Board may use administrative assistance to carry out its duties.
- (10) In  case  the  Examination  Board  of  the  partner  universities  is  responsible,  the Academic Coordinator of VGU has the responsibility to accelerate the necessary decisions of the Examination Board of the partner university.
- (11) If  students  have  to  provide  evidence  to  the  Examination  Board  of  VGU  or  the Examination Board of the partner university according to this general examination regulation, they must  provide the evidence to the Examination Office at VGU, which  notes  the  date  of  receipt  and  forwards  the  evidence  to  the  respective Examination Board.

## CHAPTER II. STRUCTURE OF STUDY

## Article 5 . Standard Period of Study, Study Scope and Structure

- (1) The standard period of study of the Bachelor's programs is at least 6 semesters and 180 CP. In order to be admitted to the Bachelor's study program, students are required to pass successfully the Foundation Year at VGU, if the Foundation Year is  not  included  in  the  curriculum  of  the  program  and  Credit  Points  are  not accumulated to the GPA.
- (2) The standard period of study of the Master's programs is normally 4 semesters and 120 CP. For part time Master's programs, this standard period can be adapted in relation to the credits given for each year.
- (3) The degree programs consist of compulsory modules, mandatory elective modules and elective modules as well as the Bachelor's thesis worth 6 to 15 CP or Master's thesis worth 15 to 30 CP. In addition, practical study units are offered.
- (4) The programs are held completely in English.

- (5) The individual exam regulations shall provide the opportunity to spend a part of the degree program in Germany.
- (6) The maximum period of study shall not exceed the standard period of the programs plus six (6) semesters for Bachelor's programs and four (4) semesters for Master's programs.

## Article 6 . Modularization

- (1) The programs are modular. Modules are thematically and chronologically rounded with self-contained units, which lead to a partial qualification in accordance with the scopes described in Article 2.
- (2) Modules are typically completed with an examination and should be studied in one semester.  In  exceptional  cases,  a  module  may  extend  over  two  semesters.  In justified exceptional cases, the module exam consists of an accumulation of several partial module examinations.
- (3) The scope of modules in credit points and their sequence are to be determined by individual examination regulations. The credit points are explained in Article 9.

## Article 7. Teaching Methods and Types of Courses

- (1) To  achieve  the  intended  learning  outcomes,  the  courses  can  be  conducted  in different forms such as for example:
2. -Lectures
3. -Preparatory courses
4. -Laboratory courses
5. -Tutorials
6. -Seminars
7. -Colloquiums
8. -Project work
9. -Internships
10. -Excursions
- (2) In lectures, the subjects of the discipline are displayed  exemplarily  and systematically and serve to impart basic and specific academic knowledge. They will provide an overview of problems in context.
- (3) Preparatory courses teach basic skills that are necessary to prepare for the study.
- (4) In laboratory  courses ,  scientific  questions  and  knowledge  are  deepened  in practical experiments and applications. As part of the laboratory courses, students, individually or in a team, acquire knowledge of laboratory work and data analyses under professional and methodological guidance.
- (5) Seminars serve to work on scientific questions and knowledge about a given topic. They are courses with active participation of students, including the processing of

a defined task and, optionally, the presentation and/or discussion of this task in an oral presentation.

- (6) Tutorials provide students with the opportunity to deal with specific issues and areas of the subject and pursuit of practical training of necessary relevant skills.
- (7) Colloquia provide  students  with  the  possibility  of  an  in-depth  discussion  of selected scientific issues and research topics, as well as to present and discuss their own research results.
- (8) Project work can be offered as part of a course or as a separate teaching method. As  part  of  project  work,  students,  individually  or  in  a  team,  acquire  scientific knowledge under professional and methodological guidance or develop concepts and  solutions  for  practical  tasks  or  for  scientific  problems  based  on  scientific methods.
- (9) Internships are  used to apply the methods of the subject and provide practical experience and insight into possible professional fields.
- (10) Excursions serve  the  immediate  confrontation  of  students  with  the  scientific subjects of the courses under academic guidance.
- (11) The teaching forms may be fully or partially offered or supported by eLearning.

## Article 8. Practical Study Elements

The study may include practical training elements such as internships. The nature, scope and learning objectives of such elements are described in the degree program specific examination regulations.

## CHAPTER III. COURSEWORK AND EXAMINATIONS

## Article 9. Credit Points (CP)

- (1) Credit Points (CP) are a quantitative measure to define the estimated workload, which is required on average to complete a module successfully.
- (2) The average workload for full-time studies that a student will spend is 1,800 work hours per academic year on average. One credit point corresponds to 30 hours of work. Thus, one academic year shall correlate to 60 CP and one semester to 30 CP.
- (3) The CP correlate to a credit in the ECTS (European Credit Transfer System).
- (4) CP may only be acquired by successfully completing module examinations.

## Article 10. Examination Forms and Deadlines

- (1) Exams are study-related, graded or ungraded module examinations and the final

thesis. For completion of an examination, students must be enrolled at the VGU. In addition, it may be necessary to enroll at the German partner university. Details are provided in the degree program specific examination regulations.

- (2) Module examinations are to be offered in temporal or factual connection with the corresponding modules.
- (3) All module examinations shall be offered at least twice a year.
- (4) The dates for  the  module  examinations  and  the  registration  procedures  will  be announced at the beginning of the first session of the module by the lecturers.
- (5) Examinations may be provided in the form of a written exam, an oral exam, a seminar, equivalent course work, written work or homework. The final form of the examination,  in  the  case  of  alternative  options  and  the  approved  aids,  are announced by the lecturers at the beginning of the semester in which the module takes place.
- (6) A written exam is an exam, in which the evidence is to be provided that within a limited time and with limited aids tasks in the field of the course referred to by the written examination, can be processed properly and relevant knowledge exists. An exam may also include the multiple-choice method. Each examination is to be evaluated and observed by an examiner as referred to in Article 16. He or she may delegate the supervision of the exam to an observer as referred to in Article 16. Written examinations, that determine the final remaining of a student in the study program, are evaluated by two examiners. The duration of an examination is based on  the  extent  of  the  module  and  is  determined  in  the  module  description.  The evaluation procedure of the examinations should not exceed 3 weeks.
- (7) In oral examinations, students should demonstrate that he or she has sufficient knowledge in the field of the examination, recognizes relationships and is able to classify them into context specific questions. Oral examinations assess students individually or in a group. They are to be held in front of an examiner and an expert assessor or two examiners. The decision about the grade is made with the consent of  both  examiners  or  the  examiner  and  expert  assessor  involved  in  the  exam. Essential objects and results of the examination are recorded according to protocol. The result of the examination is to be announced to the students following the oral examination. Students who participate in the same module and are going to be examined in the same examination can be admitted to the examination as listeners in  accordance  with  the  spatial  conditions,  unless  the  candidate  objects.  The admission does not include the presence at the consultation and announcement of the examination results. The temporal scope of the oral examination is based on the extent of the module and will be announced at the beginning of the course. The test duration is the same for all students who attend the same oral exam. The test can  be  taken  by  videoconference  if  the  examiner  belongs  to  a  German  partner university. In this case, the assessor or the second examiner shall be an academic staff member of the VGU and shall be located with the student in the same room

during the examination.

- (8) Seminars are performances of students, which are held by a candidate or a group of candidates in form of a speech or an illustrated graphic presentation in front of participants of the seminar and are marked by the lecturer. The evaluation of the seminar  has  to  be  clearly  documented  in  written  protocol.  A  seminar  may  be supplemented by a written report. The scope and specific form of the seminar is based  on  the  extent  of  the  module  and  is  announced  at  the  beginning  of  the corresponding course by the lecturer. The evaluation procedure of the examination should not exceed 3 weeks.
- (9) A  module  examination, in the form of course work  equivalent to an examination, consists of a number of contributions made on the subjects assigned to  the  module.  Such  equivalent  course  work  may,  for  example,  consist  of  oral examination,  discussions,  seminar  papers,  written  tests,  other  written  work,  or records of practical work. Course work equivalent to an examination may also be carried out in the form of group work, provided that in each case the individual contributions  to  the  overall  work  are  clearly  identifiable.  The  overall  grade  is derived  from  the  individual  grades  of  the  equivalent  course  work,  weighted  in accordance with factors, determined by the module supervisor and announced at the beginning of the first session in a module. The evaluation procedure of the examination should not exceed 3 weeks.
- (10) A written report or homework should reflect the essential facts, contexts and interpretations  of  the  subject  of  the  course,  but  should  go  beyond  the  matters discussed in the lecture. The students can suggest a topic; while the issuance of the topic is made by the lecturer, who records the date of the issue and the processing time.  All  points  of  the  written  report  or  the  homework  that  have  been  taken, literally or analogously, from public or non-public sources and therefore do not constitute original performance of the students have to be marked as such and the source has to be named. The homework or the written report has to be submitted in time with a declaration of the student that he or she has done it on his or her own and  did  not  use  unnamed  sources  or  unpermitted  aids.  Late  submissions  of homework or written reports shall be counted as a failed attempt. The evaluation of the written report and the homework shall not exceed 4 weeks. The evaluation has to be reported comprehensively according to protocol. A written report or the homework may be preceded by a seminar presentation. The scope and specific form is based on the extent of the module and announced at the beginning of the corresponding course by the lecturer.
- (11) Group work can only be approved by a lecturer, if the individual contribution of each group member can be evaluated.

## Article 11. Registration and Admission to Modules and Module Examinations

- (1) To participate in a module, including the module examination, those students who

meet the prerequisites of each course, must have not lost the right to be examined in their course of study or a course recognized as equivalent, or have already passed the examination successfully will be admitted.

- (2) The registration for a module includes the registration for the module examination. The registration will take place through an electronic system no later than one week from the beginning of the module. During this time the student can also step back from the registration.
- (3) Mandatory modules, as defined in the examination regulations of the degree, will be automatically registered for the student in the electronic registration system if he/she fulfills the requirements as defined in (1).
- (4) The  examination  regulations  of  the  degree  program  may  provide  that  the examination must be made within a specified period after taking the course or after the semester to which the course is assigned by the module handbook.
- (5) Students are allowed to take part in the module examination if the student attended at  least  75%  of  the  requested  contact  hours  of  the  module  as  described  in  the respective  module  description.  The  module  description  may  define  further requirements.
- (6) Students  can  only  unsubscribe  from  a  module  examination  for  important  and justified reasons. The reason for withdrawal must be in writing, without delay to the relevant Faculty and the Department of Academic and Student Affairs.

## Article 12. Pass and Repetition of Modules and Module Examinations

- (1) An  examination  is  passed  if  it  has  been  graded  "sufficient"  or  better.  If  an examination  has  no  grades,  it  is  passed  when  the  conditions  stipulated  in  the examination  regulations  of  the  degree  program  have  been  successfully  met.  A passed examination may not be retaken.
- (2) A module is passed if the conditions prescribed in the module description for each degree program have been fulfilled.
- (3) A module is failed if the student's academic results do not meet the requirements and  the  examination  has  been  graded  'failed'.  Then  the  student  is  required  to repeat the module examination.
- (4) Failed module examinations or partial module examinations may be repeated one time.  The repetition must be taken at the next possible examination date. Students will be automatically registered for the next possible examination date in the online registration system.
- (5) After failing an examination twice, students are allowed to retake the module one more  time.  After  retaking  the  module,  students  will  have  the  third  module examination  and  one  last  repeated  time  (if  required).  Tuition  fee  for  retaking modules are charged based on the module's credit points.

- (6) Retests in written or oral exams shall be evaluated by at least two examiners if it is the student's last attempt.
- (7) A module examination is not passed when all examination attempts according to paragraphs  (4)  and  (5)  have  failed.  Notification  of  final  failure  of  a  module examination will come as a written notice by the chairman of the Examination Board, which is to include the right of appeal.
- (8) The announcement of the grades for the individual examinations is carried out respecting data protection by public notice and within the electronic system, where one exists.
- (9) In cases of force majeure (epidemic, natural disaster), the Presidential Board may issue a separate decision on the number of examination attempts for a module.

## Article 13. Failure and Withdrawal

- (1) A module examination or partial module examination is considered as "failed" if the student does not take part in the examination without good reason or withdraws in the last week before the examination without good reason. The same applies if a written examination is not completed within the specified time, a blank sheet is given or the student remains silent in an oral exam.
- (2) The reason for the withdrawal or failure must be given to the Examination Board (via the program assistant) and made credible in writing without delay. Failure or withdrawal due to illness of the student must be proven by a medical certificate. The certificate must be issued immediately to the Examination Board and has to prove the impact on the student's ability to participate in the examination. If in doubt, a medical certificate by an independent medical examiner may be required. In case the inability to take part in the examination comes into effect during the examination,  the  student  has  to  claim  it  immediately  to  the  examiner.  The obligation to notify and make the reasons credible to the Examination Board shall remain unaffected. If the student was forced to withdraw or failure was due to illness of a child or close relatives (parents, grandparents, spouse) that he or she has to take care of, the same rights as in the case of his or her own illness apply. One good reason is the evidence of maternity protection by a student. If the reason is accepted, the next possible date can be given or a new date is set by the lecturer.
- (3) The Examination Board shall decide on the recognition of the reasons given. If the Examination Board accepts the reasons for the failure of the test in question it will not be counted as a number of attempts.

## Article 14. Additional Examinations

Students may be examined in additional modules at their own request. The results are not considered when calculating the overall grade. They are listed in the Transcript of Records.

## Article 15. Online Examination, Alternative Examination Arrangements and Legal Protection Periods

- (1) Alternative examination arrangements may be made for students with long - or short - term medical conditions, specific learning difficulties or disabilities, such that the overall requirements of academic standards are maintained.
- (2) Students who cannot participate or have to leave an examination early because of health issues are required to submit a medical certificate to the Examination Board within three days after the examination.
- (3) If a student proves, by medical certificate, that he or she is unable to take part in an  examination,  wholly  or  partially,  in  the  form  provided  due  to  ongoing  or permanent physical or mental disability or chronic illness, this disadvantage has to be  compensated  by  taking  appropriate  measures,  such  as  an  extension  of  the processing time or other procedures. However, the professional requirements must not  be  compromised.    Any  such  compensatory  measure  must  be  requested  in writing at the latest with the registration for the examination.
- (4) The legal maternity protection periods, periods of parental leave (the time off due to the care and upbringing of children), the care of the spouse or a close relative, shall be considered.
- (5) Alternative examination arrangements may be made if the student is not able to attend  an  examination  on-site  due  to  force  majeure  via  an  approved  online examination solution.
- (6) In  the  case  of  force  majeure,  when  on-site  examinations  cannot  take  place  as described in the module description of the course and online examinations must be chosen as an alternative assessment method, the Examination Board can decide whether or not to count the number of attempts, in consultation with the examiner on a case-by-case basis.

## CHAPTER IV. ORGANIZATION OF EXAMINATION

## Article 16. Examiners, Assessors

- (1) Members of the groups of professors, senior lecturers, as well as lecturers and language  teachers  are  authorized  to  evaluate  university  examinations.  Adjunct faculty members, who are in a teaching position, can be appointed as examiners for a degree program with their consent. Examinations may only be evaluated by people who possess the same level of qualification or an equivalent qualification in the respective field, which shall be ascertained by the examination.
- (2) As assessors for oral examinations, only persons may be appointed, who possess

the same level of qualification or an equivalent qualification in the respective field, which shall be ascertained by the examination. The appointment of the assessor is done  by  the  chairman  of  the  Examination  Board.  He  or  she  may  delegate  the appointment to an examiner.

- (3) In general, the module examinations are conducted by the lecturer of the module without special order by the Examination Board. If the lecturer cannot conduct the examination, the Examination Board may appoint another examiner.
- (4) The chairman of the Examination Board ensures that the names of the examiners will  be  announced  to  the  candidates  at  least  two  weeks  before  the  date  of  the examination. The principles of data protection have to be observed.
- (5) Examiners and Assessors are bound to official secrecy.
- (6) As  observers  for  written  examinations,  only  persons  may  be  appointed,  who possess  the  same  level  of  qualification  or  an  equivalent  qualification  in  the respective field, which shall be ascertained by the examination.

## Article 17. Proof Reading Rules

The University does not offer a proof-reading service to students nor does it recommend the use of any proof-reading services. Proof-reading is the final stage of producing a piece  of  written  work  and  therefore,  whenever  possible,  the  University  believes  that students  should  carry  out  their  own  proof-reading. When  a  student  chooses  to  ask another individual to proof-read their work or uses a proof-reading service to undertake this task, they must adhere to the following rules.

- (1) In all cases, work submitted by a student must be their own work and any use of a third-party proof-reader, proof-reading service or editing service must  not compromise the authorship of the work submitted.
- (2) Students should consult their respective study program policy with respect to the use of proof-readers for particular programs and modules, and ensure that they adhere to the relevant rules.
- (3) On submission of their work, students should declare if they have used a proofreader, proof-reading service or editing service.
- (4) Where a proof-reader or proof-reading service is used they may:
- a. Identify spelling and typographical errors
- b. Identify poor grammar
- c. Highlight formatting errors or inconsistencies
- d. Identify errors in labelling of diagrams, charts or figures
- e. Highlight  a  sentence  or  paragraph  that  is  overly  complex  or  where  the intended meaning is not clear
- f. Draw attention to repeated phrases or omitted words
- (5) Where a proof-reader or proof-reading service is used they must not:
- a. Rewrite passages of text to clarify the meaning
- b. Change any words or figures, only spelling corrections are accepted.

- c. Check or rewrite calculations, formulae, equations or computer code
- d. Rearrange or reformat passages of text
- e. Contribute any additional material to the original piece of written work
- f. Re-label diagrams, charts or figures
- (6) Failure to adhere to the above requirements will result in an investigation by the Examination Board, and appropriate penalties will be applied.

## Article 18. Fraud in Exams and Breach of Regulations

- (1) Cheating involves actual, intended, or attempted deception and/or dishonest action in relation to any academic work of the university. The university operates a strict policy in relation to cheating in examinations and records any dishonest action into the academic performance history of student(s). If a student is caught cheating, the examination will be graded 'failed'.
- (2) In case of serious fraud (e.g. repeated fraud or fraud with a written statement by a student that he or she did the examination on his or her own without unauthorized aids),  the  Examination  Board  can  exclude  the  student  from  taking  further examinations or coursework such that the right to continue the degree program expires. The severity of fraud shall be determined based on the effort expended such as organized cooperation and/or the type of unauthorized technical aids used, for example, electronic and smart devices.
- (3) A  student  who  disrupts  the  examination  can  be  excluded  from  continuing  the examination  by  the  examiner  or  the  examination  proctor.  In  this  case  the examination will be graded "failed".
- (4) If a student participates in an examination wrongly through culpable conduct, the Examination Board may decide that the examination is considered as 'failed'.
- (5) The student may make a written request to the Examination Board to review its decision within a 4-week period.
- (6) Where the Examination Board has deemed a student guilty of cheating, its decision has to be promptly issued, in writing, and include both justification and a course of legal action.

## CHAPTER VI. THESES AND COMPLETION OF DEGREE EXAMINATION

## Article 22. Nature and Scope of a Degree Examination

A degree examination consists of:

- successful  completion  of  all  modules  in  accordance  with  the  program-specific examination regulations;
- the final thesis and;
- optionally a colloquium supplementing the thesis.

## Article 23. Final Thesis

- (1) The final thesis should demonstrate that the student is able to work independently using scientific methods on a professional problem within a prescribed period.
- (2) The  program-specific  examination  regulations  may  define  preconditions  to  be admitted to the thesis.
- (3) The final thesis may be supervised by any suitably qualified person of the faculty as  well  as  the  German  partner  university  that  is  entitled  to  supervise  it.  The supervision  by  non-faculty  examiners  is  permissible  with  the  consent  of  the chairman of the Examination Board or his deputy.
- (4) The student must be given the opportunity to propose a thesis topic, but it may not be accepted.
- (5) The  topic  is  decided  by  the  Supervisor  and  has  to  be  acknowledged  by  the Academic Director. The date of issue shall be recorded.

- (6) The final thesis is written in English.
- (7) With the consent of the chairman of the Examination Board, the final thesis can be conducted in an institution that may not necessarily a university. In this case, the topic must be agreed in consultation with a member of the group of professors and lecturers of the responsible profession, and the thesis can only be supervised by a professor or lecturer of the faculty. An external supervisor can be appointed as the second supervisor.
- (8) The preparation time for the final thesis is three months for Bachelor's programs and six months for Master's programs, starting from the receipt of the topic, as long  as  the  program  specific  examination  regulations  do  not  provide  other preparation times. The tasks and scope of the thesis must be limited such that the deadline  for  the  thesis  can  be  met.  The  regular  scope  of  the  thesis  shall  be determined by the program specific examination regulations. Statistics, matrices, illustrations, etc. are to be incorporated into the Annex and shall not be counted when calculating the number of pages of the thesis. The examination regulations of each study program shall provide uniform formality of the thesis.
- (9) The workload for the final thesis should be between 6 and 15 credit points for Bachelor's programs, and between 15 and 30 credit points for Master's programs.
- (10) The  topic  can  only  be  changed  once,  and  only  within  the  first  four  weeks  of preparation time. In the case of repetition of the final thesis, the change of the topic is  only  permitted  if  the  student  has  not  proceeded  with  this  option  in  the  first attempt. The chairman of the Examination Board may extend the preparation time once if the student cannot meet the deadline due to circumstances beyond their control, and the student had requested an extension prior to the expiration of the deadline.  The  duration  of  the  extension  is  at  the  discretion  of  the  chairman  of Examination Board. If a student is prevented for a longer period from working on the thesis due to reasons, which are out of their control, they can withdraw from the thesis.
- (11) The final thesis must be written according to the rules of scientific practice. In particular, all references, pictures and drawings that have been taken literally or analogously from publications or from other sources have to be identified as such. The final thesis must be accompanied by a declaration from the student that he or she has written the thesis independently and without using sources/material other than the specified sources and approved aids. Further, it must be declared that the final  thesis  has  not,  even  partially,  been  used  for  any  other  examination  or coursework.
- (12) The program-specific examination regulations may provide for a colloquium. The colloquium for the final thesis represents a 30 to 45-minute discussion, in which, the candidate responds to critical questions about his or her thesis and, in addition, a theme of or question regarding the final thesis will be discussed in depth. The theme or question is agreed upon with the candidate at the time of registration for

the  colloquium.  The  colloquium  is  to  be  evaluated  by  two  examiners  or  an examiner in the presence of a competent assessor. The colloquium will only be conducted if the final thesis is graded at least 'sufficient'.

## Article 24. Evaluation and Archive of the Final Thesis

- (1) The graduating students are required to submit within a specified time the thesis package which includes:
- a. A specific number of hard-bound copies of the final thesis to their program office as defined in the examination regulations of their study program;
- b. One hard-bound copy of the final thesis to the Library of VGU;
- c. One electronic copy of the final thesis to their program office and the Library;
- d. The  thesis  submission  receipt  given  to  the  student  by  the  study  program assistant when he/she submits the thesis package. The student must provide written  confirmation  on  the  receipt  that  he  or  she  has  done  the  thesis independently, without the use of unspecified sources or aids, and that any and all citations are marked as such. The date of submission is to be recorded.

If the final thesis is not submitted in time, it is regarded as "failed".

- (2) The final thesis should be evaluated independently by two examiners. One of them is the supervisor according to article 23 (3). The second examiner is determined by the  chairman  of  the  Examination  Board.  Both  the  supervisor  and  the  examiner assign a grade. The overall grade of the final thesis is formed from the arithmetic average of both grades. If there is a difference of at least 2.0 on the German grading scale (which is 3.0 points on the Vietnamese scale), or one examiner evaluates the thesis  as  "failed"  while  the  other  evaluates  it  as  "sufficient"  or  better,  a  third examiner is appointed by the Examination Board. The overall grade is formed by the arithmetic average of the three individual grades.
- (3) The duration of the evaluation procedure should not exceed 8 weeks.
- (4) Upon submission of the final thesis, the Library has the right to publish the final thesis in hard-bound copy and on its digital collections. The publication of the final thesis  has  to  be  authorized  by  the  supervisor.  With  the  agreement  of  the examination board, the publication of the final thesis can be deferred to protect commercial property rights or for other justifiable reasons. The format and storage medium will be decided by the Library.
- (5) The study program office will store 01 hard copy and the electronic version of the final thesis as a part of the examination folder.
- (6) Passed final theses must be archived for 20 years, and failed theses for 5 years.

## Article 25. Repetition of the Final Thesis

- (1) A failed thesis can be repeated once. Another topic will be issued. A return of the topic of the thesis is permitted only as part of a re-test if the student has not made

use of this opportunity during the preparation of the first final thesis. A second repetition is not allowed.

- (2) The thesis is  finally  failed  when  the  second  attempt  is  evaluated  as  "failed"  or regarded as "failed".

## Article 26. Passing the Degree Examination and Graduation Consideration

- (1) The  degree  examination  is  passed  and  the  Master's  or  Bachelor's  degrees completed, if all the necessary modules and the thesis are successfully completed, possibly including the colloquium are, evaluated as at least "sufficient" and the required credit points were achieved.
- (2) The  overall  grade  of  the  degree  examination  is  calculated  as  the  weighted arithmetic average of all the grades and credit points of the module examinations, including the thesis.
- (3) The degree examination is finally failed if;
- a.  compulsory modules are definitively failed or

b. the thesis at the second attempt is graded as "failed" or regarded as "failed".

- (4) If the degree examination is definitively failed, the chairman of the Examination Board issues a written notice, which includes all the information about passed and failed  examinations  along  with  the  reasons  for  the  failure  to  pass  the  final examination.
- (5) If the student has finally failed the degree examination, the student is to deregister. At request, he or she receives a certificate from the examination office detailing relevant information about the passed module examinations including the grades and the acquired credit points, stating that the examination has been irrevocably failed.
- (6) The list of graduates will be distributed to the relevant departments (in the table below) to be double checked and for the approval of completion before the degree can be issued to the students.

## Article 27.   Temporary Suspension, Withdrawal of Studies and Program Transfer

- (1) Students  who  are  engaged  in  military  service,  or  who  have  health  problems, financial  difficulty,  urgent  family  affairs  or  other  personal  reasons,  may  be permitted  to  suspend  their  studies  for  a  period  of  up  to  four  semesters.  The suspension period will be included in the maximum allowable period of study at VGU except in cases of suspension due to military service and (long term treatment of) health problems.
- (2) Students shall submit all supporting documents together with an explanatory letter for suspension of studies to the Presidential Board via the Department of Academic and Student Affairs.
- (3) The Presidential Board decides on the application and informs students of their decision. Successful applicants will be notified of the start and end  date of the period of suspension. All the examination results of students are to be retained, including passed and failed modules.
- (4) Upon expiration of the suspension period, the student may only be permitted to return to his/her studies by submitting an application for resumption of studies at least one week before the start of the new semester. If the student fails to submit a report  to  the  Department  of  Academic  and  Student  Affairs  according  to  the schedule, he/she will be classified as withdrawn.
- (5) Students  intending  to  leave  the  University  prior  to  graduation  must  apply  for deregistration to the Department of Academic and Student Affairs. A student who leaves the University without following the proper procedures will be considered as unofficially withdrawn and no official documents will be issued to the student. A transcript of records upon approval of deregistration will only be issued after complete clearance of all responsibilities with VGU.
- (6) If the student re-applies to study at VGU after an official withdrawal, he or she must again pass the university's current admission procedures.
- (7) Students of Bachelor programs can make a request to the Department of Academic and  Student  Affairs  to  transfer  to  another  study  program  only  once  during  the standard  period  of  study  of  their  study  program  (refer  to  Article  5).  Any  such request will be reviewed by the Academic Coordinators of both study programs before submitting to the Presidential Board. If the request is finally approved by the Presidential Board, those accepted students must pay the fees for and complete the workload of any missed modules of the program they are joining.
- (8) Students must meet the admission requirements of the program to which he/she wishes to enroll.
- (9) Students  must  have  enough  time  to  complete  all  of  their  new  study  program requirements without exceeding the maximum allowable period of study for the

Bachelor's degree (refer to Article 5).

## Article 28. Invalidity of the Examination

- (1) If a student has cheated in any part of an examination, and if this becomes known only after  the  award  of  the  certificate,  the  Examination  Board  must  amend  the grades so affected retrospectively and will declare that the entire examination or part thereof has not been passed.
- (2) If the conditions governing the right to take an examination have not been fulfilled although it was not the candidate's deliberate intention to mislead the Examination Board, and if this fact should become known only after the award of the degree, the situation will be compensated for by the passing of the examination itself. If a candidate has deliberately used deception in order to be admitted, the Examination Board, with due reference to general administrative principles, will decide whether or not to revoke administrative documents acquired illegally.
- (3) A candidate shall  be  accorded  the  opportunity  to  make  a  statement  before  any decision is made in accordance with paragraph 1 and 2.
- (4) An  examination  certificate that is incorrect will be retracted and,  where appropriate, a replacement will be issued.

- (1) Degree awarding, amendment and withdrawal have to be documented in a degree register in VGU's management system. Copies of certificates and degrees must be archived  for  an  unlimited  period  of  time  by  the  Department  of  Academic  and Student  Affairs.  Other  documents  such  as  module  recognition  records  must  be archived for ten years.
- (2) After completion of their study program, the graduates are entitled upon request to inspect their examination records (i.e. written examinations, the related reports of the examiners and the examination protocols).
- (3) A request under paragraph 2 made to the chairman of the Examination Board can only be accepted within one year from graduation. The Department of Academic and Student Affairs determines the time and place of the inspection and conducts the inspection in the presence of the student.

## Article 32. Transitional Provisions

The  general  examination  regulations  shall  apply  to  all  specific  examination regulations that are adopted or amended after the adoption of this general examination regulation.

## Article 33. Taking Effect

- (1) This general examination regulation comes into effect on the date it is signed.

- (2) Faculty Staff, Academic Coordinators and Heads of the relevant Departments are responsible for implementing, guiding, checking and monitoring the implementation of this regulation.
- (3) If  there  are  any  issues  that  require  attention  during  the  execution  of  these regulations, the relevant units and individuals will report them to the Presidential Board  via  the  Department  of  Academic  and  Student  Affairs  for  revision, modification or addendum./.
